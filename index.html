HACK for education

Not sure where to start? Here are some challenges to get you going:

Addressing Accessibility and Social/Culture practices:

  • How might we create tools for the indigent communities instead of having students go to the schools.
  • How might we create alternative learning programs that can reach students in far flung areas.
  • How might we create tools to prevent girls from dropping out of school due to teenage pregnancy, sexual harassment and early marriages.

    • Addressing Financial Issues:

  • How might we create tools to prevent Child Labor, or enforce more stringent policies about child workers.
  • How might we create tools to provide Children with the materials they need to attend school.
  • How might we create Alternative Learning Programs that eliminate the need for additional expenses.

    • Addressing Problems on Resource:

  • How might we create tools to improve the transparency of public funding to schools.
  • How might we create tools to provide updated on school materials.
  • How might we create tools to digitalise learning materials as a back up for when the existing educational materials are unavailable.

    • Case Studies

    These scenarios below describe composite examples of children before and after missing out on education. Use the scenarios in your design process to ensure that what you build responds to real needs.

    Prevention Case Scenarios

    Dalisay is 14 years old living in the province of Camarines Sur. She is currently living in poverty. Her family can not afford to send her to college. At an early age she has to start work to support her family. She does not see school as necessary because she can earn money through other means. She has left secondary school without having mastered required levels of literacy and numeracy.

    Reaction Case Scenarios

    Christ is a 8 years old child from the Bateke minority in Democratic Republic of the Congo. He lives in katanga province which is one of the most violent yet neglected regions of the Congo. He has a dream to become a teacher one day, but he is too afraid to go to school. Some of his friends were abducted on their way to school. They were forced into child labour.

    Judging Criteria

    Viability

  • Does it respond to one of the formulated use-cases ?
  • Is it financially viable and feasible ?
  • What is the potential for scale-up ?
  • Is the MVP, or idea, applicable to other industries ?

    • Originality

  • Level of innovation - How disruptive is the MVP ?
  • Is it a technical and/or a business model innovation ?
  • How creative has the group been in solving the challenge ?
  • Did they build up on existing solutions, or came up with out-of-the-box concepts ?
  • How creative was the group in overcoming barriers to entry ?

    • Execution

  • Can the group show the MVP ?
  • Does the idea come to live with the demo ?
  • How well designed is the MVP ?
  • Is the hack testable at minimum cost ?

    • Sustainability

  • Does it respect gender equality ? The environment ? Cultural differences ?
  • Is there a potential to pivot towards another SDG ?
